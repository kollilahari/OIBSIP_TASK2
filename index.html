Dr. Stephen Hawking

Author, Cosmologist and Theoretical Physicist

Stephen Hawking as a young man

Here's a detailed timeline of Dr. Hawking's life:

  • 1942 - Born on January 8th in Oxford, England, to Frank and Isobel Hawking.
  • 1950 - Family moves to St Albans, Hertfordshire. He attends St Albans school.
  • 1959 - Awarded a scholarship at Oxford University and completes his undergraduate studies in just 4 years.
  • 1962 - Begins his graduate work at Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1963 - Diagnosed with motor neurone disease (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, ALS) at the age of 21.
  • 1965 - Marries Jane Wilde, whom he had met shortly before his diagnosis.
  • 1967 - Birth of his first child, Robert.
  • 1969 - Birth of his daughter, Lucy.
  • 1970 - Receives his PhD degree in applied mathematics and theoretical physics, specializing in general relativity and cosmology. His essay titled "Singularities and the Geometry of Space-Time" wins the prestigious Adams Prize.
  • 1973 - Publishes his first book, "The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time," co-authored with George Ellis.
  • 1974 - Birth of his second son, Timothy.
  • 1975 - Moves back to Cambridge and becomes a reader in gravitational physics.
  • 1979 - His speech deteriorates, requiring others to translate his speech into intelligible words.
  • 1982 - Completes the first draft of his popular-science book, "A Brief History of Time," which sells millions of copies worldwide.
  • 1985 - Undergoes a tracheotomy, which significantly affects his ability to speak but continues his groundbreaking research.
  • 1990 - Publishes "A Brief History of Time," which becomes an international bestseller.
  • 1992 - Appears as himself in an episode of "Star Trek: The Next Generation."
  • 1995 - Divorces Jane Hawking and marries Elaine Mason.
  • 2004 - Announces his retirement as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge University.
  • 2009 - Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian honor in the United States.
  • 2015 - States his theory that information might be retrievable from a black hole, contrary to previous beliefs.
  • 2018 - Passes away on March 14th, leaving behind a legacy of scientific breakthroughs and inspiration.
